# 👋 About Me

I am an Applied Research Scientist at [ServiceNow](https://www.servicenow.com/), where I work on **AI agents for enterprise automation**. My research studies how large language models can reliably interact with complex enterprise systems to execute real-world workflows. I design **evaluation environments and benchmarks for enterprise tasks**, and investigate how different classes of agents—from web-based agents operating through graphical interfaces to programmatic agents interacting directly with APIs and documentation—can solve challenging automation problems. My goal is to develop **reliable agent architectures and evaluation frameworks** that enable practical AI automation in real-world enterprise environments.

At ServiceNow, I have contributed to several initiatives applying machine learning and language models to enterprise automation. This includes research on **text-to-workflow generation** and **image-to-workflow generation** for [ServiceNow Flow Designer](https://www.servicenow.com/products/platform-flow-designer.html), as well as systems that automate IT service management tasks such as [**issue auto-resolution**](https://www.servicenow.com/docs/bundle/washingtondc-servicenow-platform/page/administer/virtual-agent/concept/auto-resolution-va.html). My work has involved building datasets and benchmarks, training domain-adapted models, and deploying AI capabilities that help organizations automate operational workflows more efficiently.

Before joining ServiceNow, I was a Data Scientist at [Intact Financial Corporation](https://www.intact.com/en/), the largest property and casualty insurer in Canada. There, I worked on natural language processing and conversational AI systems for insurance applications. My work included analyzing call center conversations, modeling customer intents, assisting support agents during interactions, and applying deep learning techniques to telematics and insurance data.

## 🎓🤖🪐 Education

I hold a Master’s degree in Machine Learning from [MILA - Quebec AI Institute](https://mila.quebec/en/), and a Bachelor’s degree in Physics from [Université de Montréal](https://phys.umontreal.ca/english/home/). During my studies, I worked as a teaching assistant for courses in special relativity and briefly contributed to astrophysics research at the [Centre de Recherche en Astrophysique du Québec (CRAQ)](https://craq-astro.ca/?lang=en).
