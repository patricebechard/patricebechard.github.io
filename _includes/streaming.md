# 👋 About Me

I am an Applied Research Scientist at [ServiceNow](https://www.servicenow.com/), where I work on **AI agents for enterprise automation**. My research focuses on how large language models can reliably interact with enterprise platforms to execute complex operational workflows. I design **realistic environments and benchmarks for enterprise tasks**, and study how different types of agents (from web-based agents operating through graphical interfaces to programmatic agents interacting directly with APIs and documentation) can solve challenging automation problems. My goal is to develop robust evaluation frameworks and practical agent systems that enable reliable AI automation in real-world enterprise environments.

At ServiceNow, I have contributed to several initiatives applying machine learning and language models to enterprise automation. This includes research on **text-to-workflow generation** and **image-to-workflow generation** for [ServiceNow Flow Designer](https://www.servicenow.com/products/platform-flow-designer.html), and systems that automate IT service management tasks such as [**issue auto-resolution**](https://www.servicenow.com/docs/bundle/washingtondc-servicenow-platform/page/administer/virtual-agent/concept/auto-resolution-va.html). My work has involved building datasets and benchmarks, training domain-adapted models, and deploying AI capabilities that help organizations automate operational workflows more efficiently.


Before joining ServiceNow, I was a Data Scientist at [Intact Financial Corporation](https://www.intact.com/en/), he largest property and casualty insurer in Canada. There, I focused on natural language processing and conversational AI for insurance applications. I worked on systems that analyze call center conversations, identify customer intents, and assist agents during support interactions, as well as projects applying deep learning to telematics and insurance data.

## 🎓🤖🪐 Education

I hold a Master’s degree in Machine Learning from [MILA - Quebec AI Institute](https://mila.quebec/en/), and a Bachelor’s degree in Physics from [Université de Montréal](https://phys.umontreal.ca/english/home/).
